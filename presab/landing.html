How It Works

Three steps. Zero manual work.

From setup to live recognition in under an hour. No complex IT infrastructure required.

01

Upload Photos

The school uploads one photo per student or staff member through the Presab admin portal. Any photo works — ID cards, WhatsApp photos, school records. The AI extracts a face signature and stores it securely.

02

Install the Device

A small edge device (Raspberry Pi or mini PC) with a USB camera is mounted at the classroom door or school gate. It runs the Presab engine locally — no internet needed for recognition.

03

Walk In. Done.

Students enter. The camera sees them. Presab matches the face in under a second and marks them present with a timestamp. The principal sees a live dashboard. Teachers see nothing change.

04

Export & Report

At the end of the day, export a full attendance CSV or let Presab auto-email the report to administrators. Absent students are flagged automatically.

Built for real Caribbean infrastructure.

Most schools don't have enterprise-grade networks. Presab was designed knowing this. The recognition engine runs entirely on the edge device — no cloud call needed to mark someone present.

The device syncs with the central dashboard over local Wi-Fi when available. If the internet goes down, it keeps running and uploads when connectivity returns.

One USB camera. One small device. That's the entire hardware requirement. No rewiring, no construction, no IT department needed.

🔒 Privacy-first: Face photos are processed locally into mathematical encodings. Raw images are never stored permanently. Encodings alone cannot reconstruct a face.
