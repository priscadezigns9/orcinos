Liquidity Concepts

Liquidity is the fuel for the market. It represents the pools of buy and sell orders that banks and institutions use to fill their large positions.

Smart Money Logic

Price seeks liquidity. It moves from one pool of liquidity to another. Identifying where retail stop losses are clustered is the key to predicting institutional expansion.

Order Blocks & FVG

An Order Block is a specific candle or price area where institutional players have placed massive orders, leaving a "footprint" in the market.

Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

When price moves too fast, it creates an imbalance. This is an FVG. The market almost always returns to "fill" these gaps before continuing its trend.

Session Psychology

Each trading session (London, New York, Asia) has its own DNA and psychological profile.

The London Judas Swing

The London open often creates a fake move (the "Judas") to trap retail traders before the true expansion of the day begins.
